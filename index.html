What the Furry Fandom is

The furry fandom is a subculture interested in anthropomorphic animal characters.

Examples of anthropomorphic attributes include exhibiting human intelligence and facial expressions,

speaking, walking on two legs, and wearing clothes. The specific term furry fandom was being used in fanzines as early as 1983,

and had become the standard name for the genre by the mid-1990s,

when it was defined as "the organized appreciation and dissemination of art and prose regarding ‘Furries’,

or fictional mammalian anthropomorphic characters.

The furry fandom is global, with hundreds of local groups existing worldwide and dozens of conventions held annually.
